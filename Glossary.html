Glossary

Attack damage: physical damage done to minions, neutral camps and objectives, and champions. Buy armor to reduce the damage you take from attack damage.
Ability power: magic damage done to minions, neutral camps and objectives, and champions. Buy magic resist to reduce the damage you take from ability power.
Burst: deal a large amount of damage in a very short amount of time.
Camps: neutral monsters found in the jungle that jungles kill in order to gain experience and gold.
Champion: a character that is controlled by a human.
Crowd control: the ability to impair the movement and/or usage of abilities of minions, neutral camps &lar;Excluding dragon and baron) and champions.
Dueling: fighting an enemy champion one on one.
Gank or roam: when a jungler (gank) or laner (roam) enters a lane, giving a numbers advantage and allowing that team to secure a kill on an enemy champion with more ease. Ganks are also meant to be a surprise, reducing the amount of time the enemy has to react.
Gold: an in-game currency that allows champions to buy items which enhance the champion.
Keystone: the strongest and most important rune in a rune page.
Mana: most champions use this as a cost for abilities.
Neutral: will only attack if it is being attacked. Neutral camps or objectives can also attack either team.
Peeling: protecting your ADC or midlaner with crowd control, heals and shields.
Recall: channel for 8 seconds to return to base.
Rune: a special set of enhancements chosen by the player before the game to aid their champion in-game.
Scaling: when a champion becomes stronger and stronger as the game progresses.
Skirmishes: an unplanned fight, especially in small groups.
Snowball: using a lead to create a larger lead, similar to how a snowball will become bigger and bigger as it rolls down a snow covered hill.
Wards: a ward is used to gain and/or maintain vision of a certain area, and is crucial to a high level of play.
XP: an abbreviation for experience used to level up your champion.
